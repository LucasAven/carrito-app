# Auth emails via custom SMTP with Spanish, app-styled templates

Supabase Auth sends signup confirmation, password recovery, magic link, and email-change messages, and we route them through a custom SMTP provider using Spanish, app-styled HTML templates rather than the built-in email service and its default English templates. The built-in service is unusable in production: it is capped at 2 emails per hour and refuses to deliver to any address that is not a project team member, so a real Operator (e.g. the owner's mother signing up) silently receives nothing. Custom SMTP lifts both limits. For our scale (a family business, single-digit signups) we use a dedicated Gmail account with an App Password: it is free, needs no owned domain because mail is sent as `@gmail.com` so Google's own SPF/DKIM cover deliverability, and it fits the "no credit card, $0 spend" constraint from [ADR-0001](./0001-always-online.md) and the v1 plan. We rejected the built-in service (team-only, 2/hour, no SLA), and we defer Resend/Brevo because, while their free tiers are larger and give a branded `no-reply@` From address, they require verifying a custom domain we do not own; the SMTP config form is identical, so swapping later is a few-minute change. The templates live in `supabase/templates/*.html` (warm cream card, brand-orange wordmark, green pill CTA, inline table layout for email-client compatibility, light mode only since email dark mode is unreliable) and are committed for version control and local-dev parity. Critically, neither the templates nor the SMTP block nor the Site URL in `supabase/config.toml` push to the hosted project: `config.toml` is local-dev only, so the production project must be configured in the Supabase Dashboard (URL Configuration, Email Templates, SMTP Settings), which is the single most common way these settings drift out of sync. Revisit when signups outgrow Gmail's ~500/day limit or its bulk-sending ToS becomes a real risk, or when we acquire a custom domain and want a branded sender, at which point move to Resend.
