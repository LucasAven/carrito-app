# The app is a fixed-height shell and each view owns its scroll

The whole app renders inside a fixed-height shell: `body` is `h-dvh overflow-hidden` (dvh, not vh, so the height tracks the visible viewport as mobile browser chrome shows and hides) and never scrolls itself. The Appbar (top) and the BottomNav plus quick-entry buttons (bottom) are `fixed`, outside normal flow; the single content area between them (rendered by `components/MainShell.tsx` inside `<main>`) is the one scroll container (`overflow-y-auto`), so any page scrolls only when its content actually overflows. This replaced a `body { min-h-screen }` model where pages stacked their own min-heights on top of the `<main>` padding (the login screen used `min-h-[80vh]`), pushing total content past `100vh` and producing a phantom scrollbar even on short pages. Because the content area, not the body, is now the scroll root, two views lean on it deliberately. **Balance** keeps the DatePicker and the Balance card pinned and scrolls only the entry list: a `flex` / `min-h-0` / `flex-1` chain runs from `MainShell` down through `BalanceView` to the `overflow-y-auto` wrapper around `EarnsCostsTab`, so the Operator always sees the period total and can change dates while moving through entries. **Stats** ("Resumen", see [v2-stats-plan](../v2-stats-plan.md)) makes its year/metric controls `sticky -top-2`. The negative `top` cancels the content area's `pt-2` so the bar pins flush under the Appbar, which is necessary because the sticky now resolves against the content scroll container, not the viewport. That `-top-2` is coupled to the container's `pt-2`: change one and the other must follow. Since the shell no longer pads the body, bottom clearance for the fixed controls is a per-route margin that `MainShell` sets from the pathname (`/balance` gets `mb-32` to clear the Venta/Gasto buttons above the nav, `/stats` gets `mb-16`, auth pages get none so the form stays centered). The same refactor settled the home view: there is no standalone landing page, so `/` redirects to `/balance` (the ledger is home), the empty Home page was deleted, and the Appbar logo points at `/balance`; unauthenticated visitors are bounced to `/login` by the middleware before they reach it (see [CONTEXT](../../CONTEXT.md), where the Balance ledger is the Operator's daily surface). We rejected keeping the body-scroll model (it cannot pin the Balance header or internal-scroll its list without per-element sticky hacks, and it reintroduces the phantom scroll), and rejected giving Balance/Stats a fixed-height `calc(100dvh - offsets)` that hardcodes the Appbar height and `<main>` padding (fragile coupling to those values) in favor of the flex `min-h-0` chain, which needs no magic numbers. Revisit if a future view needs the entire viewport to scroll as a single column: it can opt out by not constraining its subtree, letting its content grow within the one scroll container.
